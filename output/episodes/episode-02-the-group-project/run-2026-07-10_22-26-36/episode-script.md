## Synopsis

Two weeks into life at Crestwood, Wren's survival system is working exactly as designed — until Mr. Alvarez pairs her with Alex, the guarded kid nobody at Maya's table has bothered to actually meet, for a term project. When the lunch table turns the pairing into a joke, Wren has to decide, fast and repeatedly, whether her spot at that table is worth more than her word. A library session, a surprise in-class share-out, and one very direct question from Alex later, Wren learns that 'fitting in' and 'telling the truth' don't always fit in the same sentence — and that the version of herself she picks now is the one that sticks.

**Themes:** Peer Pressure, Honesty, Teamwork

## Cast

- **Wren Okafor-Silva** (`player`, 13, she/her) — Player-character protagonist of 'First Bell.' Her practiced first-day survival system collides with real choices — who to sit with, who to defend, who to become — forcing her to notice, maybe for the first time, that the performance has a cost.

- **Maya Delacroix-Thompson** (`maya`, 13, she/her) — Maya is the gravitational center of the popular table and the primary test of the player's spine — not a villain, but a mirror of what Wren's own survival instincts could calcify into if she never risks anything. - **Jordan Oyelaran** (`jordan`, 13, he/him) — The warm first contact who represents 'belonging' as ease and popularity-by-proximity — a low-stakes ally whose limits (avoiding real conflict) surface right when the player needs someone to hold ground, making room for the player's own growth to matter more.

Alex is not a lesson in tolerance; they're a specific, guarded kid testing whether this new place will go the way the last one did. Every branch of the episode is really a referendum on whether Wren notices Alex, and what she does with that noticing — from a passing glance in the hallway to a real, if fragile, friendship forged under pressure in the library.
